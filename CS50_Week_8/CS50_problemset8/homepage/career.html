I'm an aspiring tech entrepreneur with a passion for building and problem-solving. My journey began in high school as a cashier, but I quickly transitioned into entrepreneurship by flipping furniture. I sourced, refurbished, and sold used furniture through online marketplaces, handling everything from logistics to customer service. This hands-on experience taught me the value of grit, creativity, and customer focus.

Over time, I realized my true passion lies not just in refurbishing, but in building—especially in the digital world. I made the decision to pivot, taking a full-time job as a Metro Banker while studying computer science. I'm currently enrolled in Harvard's CS50: Introduction to Computer Science course, and I’m working toward a tech apprenticeship to deepen my skills and gain real-world experience.

My goal is to launch my own tech company—one that solves meaningful problems and creates lasting impact. I'm always looking to connect with like-minded individuals, mentors, and opportunities in the tech space.
